German auxiliary verbs are essential for forming compound tenses and expressing actions in the past, present, and future. The three main auxiliary verbs are "sein" (to be), "haben" (to have), and "werden" (to become), which help to provide context and meaning in sentences.

## Functions of Auxiliary Verbs

### Sein (to be)
- Used with verbs of motion or change.
- Example: "Ich bin gelaufen." (I ran.)

### Haben (to have)
- Commonly used with transitive verbs (verbs that take a direct object).
- Example: "Sie hat eine Pizza gegessen." (She ate a pizza.)

### Werden (to become)
- Used to form the future tense and passive voice.
- Example: "Wir werden morgen reisen." (We will travel tomorrow.)

## Conjugation of Auxiliary Verbs

### Present Tense Conjugation

|Person|Sein|Haben|Werden|
|---|---|---|---|
|ich|bin|habe|werde|
|du|bist|hast|wirst|
|er/sie/es|ist|hat|wird|
|wir|sind|haben|werden|
|ihr|seid|habt|werdet|
|sie/Sie|sind|haben|werden|

### Past Tense Conjugation
